AI Readiness 3×5 Framework

Your AI Readiness Roadmap
A 3x5 Framework for new Life-Sci Grads

3 Pillars Complexity (depth), Integration (workflow), Scope (reach).

5 Stages Personal → Team → Department → Cross‑functional → Org.

From first prompts to team impact—fast. Start simple, prove ROI, learn with confidence.

The 3 Pillars

Complexity

No‑code → Low‑code → Custom

Choose the simplest implementation that consistently saves time. Move to low‑code and custom services only when metrics justify the jump.

  1. No‑code: prompts, templates, spreadsheets
  2. Low‑code: automation builders (Zapier/Make)
  3. Custom: services/APIs where ROI is proven

Integration

One‑off → Process → System

Evolve from one‑offs to process‑bound SOPs, then to system‑level automation with triggers, alerts, and audit trails.

  1. One‑off tasks (drafts, summaries)
  2. Process‑bound (SOPs, checklists, reviews)
  3. System‑level (triggers, alerts, audits)

Scope

Personal → Org

Expand the audience of your workflow while preserving quality and governance: from personal use to team, department, cross‑functional and organization‑wide adoption.

  1. Personal -> Team
  2. Department -> Cross‑functional
  3. Cross‑functional -> Org‑wide

The 5 Stages

1

Stage 1 — Personal: Daily Reps

Use cases: Quickly summarize research papers; draft clear, concise emails and reports; turn dense SOPs into simple checklists.

Metric: Focus on one thing: how much time you save per task. Aim for 20-30%.

Tools: ChatGPT Claude Perplexity
2

Stage 2 — Team: Standardize Wins

Use cases: Build a shared library of high-quality prompts; create smart templates in Notion or Google Docs that your whole team can use.

Metric: See your work get adopted. Track how often your templates are used.

Tools: Notion Google Docs Slack
3

Stage 3 — Department: Automate & Connect

Use cases: Connect your tools: auto-file documents from emails, send Slack alerts when a tracker is updated, or create meeting digests.

Guardrails: Learn responsible automation: versioning, protecting sensitive data, and keeping an audit log.

Tools: n8n Airtable Zapier Openrouter Custom Scripts
4

Stage 4 — Cross‑Functional: Handoffs & SLAs

Use cases: Create reliable handoffs between teams, like summarizing clinical data for the regulatory team or enriching KOL profiles for marketing.

Metrics: Focus on reliability. Your goal is to reduce errors and hit your service-level agreements (SLAs).

Tools: Proprietary Services Custom Scripts
5

Stage 5 — Org: Enterprise Moats

Use cases: Now you're building systems: proprietary knowledge bases, evidence pipelines, and dashboards that help leaders make decisions.

Metric: The big picture: measure the dollar impact of your work and how it's reused across the business.

Tools: Proprietary Services Custom Scripts

Your First Year: A Growth Roadmap

  • First 3 Months (The Efficiency Expert): Your goal is personal productivity. Master prompting and find one or two daily tasks you can do 50% faster. Target: Save yourself 5 hours a week.
  • Months 3-6 (The Team Player): Start sharing your wins. Turn a successful workflow into a template or SOP your immediate team can use. Target: Get one reusable asset adopted by your team.
  • Months 6-12 (The Connector): Begin automating across tools. Connect an email insight to a tracker, or a spreadsheet update to a Slack channel. Target: Build one automated workflow that saves your team time.
  • Year 2 (The Problem Solver): Look for bigger opportunities. Find a manual process between two teams and build a reliable, automated handoff. Target: Solve one cross-functional problem.

Measuring Success: Metrics That Matter

  • Time Saved (Efficiency): The simplest starting metric. How many minutes/hours are you saving per task?
  • Adoption Rate (Utility): Are people using what you built? Track the usage of your templates and workflows.
  • Cycle Time (Speed): How long does a process take from start to finish? Measure the reduction in key departmental workflows.
  • Defect Rate (Quality): As you automate, is quality improving? Track errors, rework, and deviations in your processes.
  • Dollar Impact (Value): The ultimate goal. Connect your work to cost savings, revenue generation, or risk reduction.

Starter Projects by Department

  • Clinical Ops: Automate trial site feasibility reports; draft initial IRB submission documents; summarize patient safety narratives.
  • R&D / Discovery: Triage and categorize new research papers; generate hypotheses from literature data; draft sections of grant proposals.
  • Business Development: Create pre-meeting briefings on potential partner companies; summarize call transcripts; generate personalized outreach emails.
  • Marketing: Draft social media posts based on recent publications; generate first-draft blog content from technical docs; summarize market research reports.
  • Product: Summarize user feedback from multiple sources into themes; draft requirements for new features; analyze competitor release notes.
